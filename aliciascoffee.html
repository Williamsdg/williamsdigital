Our Story

A Healing Place
for All

Founded in 2020 by Naimah Alicia Elmore and Rashad Harrell, Alicia's Coffee was born from a simple belief: coffee shops are healing places — safe spaces for people of all ages and backgrounds to come together, share stories, and build connection.

Nestled inside the historic A.G. Gaston Motel — built in 1954 and designated a Birmingham Civil Rights National Monument by President Obama in 2017 — we honor the legacy of community and courage that defines this sacred space.

Selected by Birmingham's Historic Preservation Authority to operate at the motel, we serve every cup with intention, guided by our founding principle:

“Coffee is a connection.”

Our Blends

Three Signature Origins

Each blend tells a story of careful sourcing and organic, responsibly grown beans with unique flavors.

Brazilian

Rich, smooth, and full-bodied with notes of chocolate and toasted nuts. Our most comforting blend.

Costa Rican

Bright and clean with a honey-like sweetness and delicate citrus undertones. A morning favorite.

Colombian

Balanced and versatile with caramel sweetness and a smooth, lingering finish. An everyday essential.

Signature Drink

The Room 30

Our handcrafted signature latte — oat milk, two shots of espresso, and raspberry syrups — named in honor of the room where Rev. Martin Luther King Jr. planned the Birmingham campaign during his stays at the A.G. Gaston Motel.

Every sip carries the spirit of courage, connection, and community that defines this historic space.

The Legacy

A Monument to Courage

The A.G. Gaston Motel, built in 1954 by entrepreneur A.G. Gaston, served as a meeting place for civil rights leaders including Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. Restored with a $10 million city investment and support from the Mellon Foundation, it stands today as a Birmingham Civil Rights National Monument — and we're proud to call it home.
