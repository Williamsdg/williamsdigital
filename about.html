Our Story

From a humble coffee cart to Birmingham's beloved community roaster. Every cup we pour carries a story of passion, community, and the relentless pursuit of great coffee.

The Beginning

How It All Started

Cala Coffee Co. began the way all good things do — with a dream, a used espresso machine, and a stubborn belief that Birmingham deserved better coffee.

What started as a weekend coffee cart at the local farmers market quickly grew into something bigger. The lines got longer, the conversations deeper, and the coffee better with every batch we roasted.

Today, with two thriving locations and a growing community of coffee lovers, we're still guided by the same simple mission: roast great coffee, serve it with heart, and build a space where everyone belongs.

Roasted With Intention

We source our beans directly from farmers and cooperatives who share our values. Every relationship is built on transparency, fair pricing, and mutual respect.

Our roasting approach is simple: honor the origin. We roast in small batches to bring out the unique character of each coffee — the blueberry sweetness of an Ethiopian natural, the chocolate richness of a Guatemalan washed lot.

We believe specialty coffee should be approachable, never pretentious. Whether you're a seasoned pour-over enthusiast or just discovering that coffee can taste like something beyond "coffee," we're here for you.

The Roastery

What We Stand For

Three principles guide everything we do.

Community First

We exist because of the people who walk through our doors. Every decision we make starts with our community. From hiring locally to supporting neighborhood causes, Birmingham is our home and we show up for it.

Quality Without Compromise

From sourcing to roasting to serving, we never cut corners. We pay above market price for exceptional beans, roast in small batches for freshness, and train our baristas to craft every cup with care.

Ethical Sourcing

Great coffee starts at the farm. We build direct relationships with producers, pay fair prices, and invest in sustainable practices. When the farmers thrive, the coffee gets better for everyone.
